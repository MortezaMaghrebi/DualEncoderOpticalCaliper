# Dual Encoder Optical Caliper

High precision optical thickness measurement mechanism using dual encoders, spring-loaded passive clamping, and lead screw driven opening system.

---

## Overview

This project is a compact high-precision mechanical measurement module designed for automatic optical lens and glass thickness measurement systems.

The mechanism uses a symmetric dual-arm structure combined with rotary encoders, timing belts, preload springs, and a lead screw driven opening system to achieve accurate and repeatable thickness measurements with low backlash and smooth motion transfer.

The system is intended for integration into automatic lens edging and CNC optical processing machines.

---

## Mechanical Concept

The mechanism consists of two curved outer arms that contact the glass surface from both sides.

Each outer arm is connected to an internal linkage arm that acts as a motion amplification and precision transfer mechanism.
These longer internal arms improve measurement sensitivity and reduce the effect of backlash or mechanical tolerances.

The internal arms are connected to timing belts.

Each belt:

* attaches to the end of an internal arm
* wraps around a rotary encoder pulley
* connects to a preload tension spring fixed to the frame

The spring continuously pulls the mechanism toward the closed position, generating controlled passive clamping force.

A central stepper motor with a lead screw mechanism actively opens the arms against the spring force.

As the arms move, the timing belts rotate the encoders, allowing precise displacement measurement.
