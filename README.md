# Dual Encoder Precision Caliper

Compact high-precision displacement measurement mechanism using dual rotary encoders, spring-loaded passive clamping, and lead screw driven actuation.

---

## Overview

This project is a compact precision measurement module designed for accurate thickness and position sensing using a symmetric mechanical linkage system.

The mechanism combines rotary encoders, timing belts, preload springs, and a lead screw driven opening structure to achieve smooth motion transfer, high repeatability, and low-backlash measurement performance.

The design focuses on compactness, mechanical precision, durability, and stable force distribution.

---

## Mechanical Concept

The mechanism consists of two curved outer arms positioned symmetrically on both sides of the device.

These outer arms act as the primary contact elements and move toward or away from each other in a controlled and repeatable motion path.

Each outer arm is connected to a longer internal linkage arm.

The internal linkage arms are critical parts of the mechanism and must remain long relative to the outer arms.
They are not decorative supports; they function as motion amplification and precision transfer elements.

The longer geometry of the internal arms increases sensitivity and improves measurement resolution by translating small outer arm movement into larger controlled internal displacement while reducing backlash influence.

Each internal arm is connected to a timing belt.

The belt routing is extremely important:

* One side of the belt is attached directly to the end of the internal linkage arm
* The belt then wraps around a precision rotary encoder pulley
* After rotating around the encoder pulley, the other end of the belt connects to a preload tension spring fixed to the main frame

This configuration creates a constant closing force while simultaneously converting arm displacement into highly accurate encoder rotation.

The preload spring continuously pulls the mechanism toward the closed position.

A central stepper motor combined with a lead screw mechanism actively pushes the linkage system open against the spring force.

The motor is responsible only for opening movement.
Closing motion is passive and generated by spring tension.

As the arms move, the belts rotate the encoders, producing precise displacement data from both sides independently.

The mechanism is designed to maintain:

* smooth synchronized motion
* low friction
* minimal hysteresis
* high repeatability
* stable contact force
* low vibration sensitivity

## Key Design Requirements

* Preserve long internal linkage arms
* Avoid shortening the amplification geometry
* Maintain belt-to-encoder coupling
* Maintain passive spring-loaded closing behavior
* Compact but mechanically rigid housing
* Minimal backlash in all pivot points
* Smooth curved outer arms
* Precision bearing-supported joints
* Clean industrial mechanical appearance
